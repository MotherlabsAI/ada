# ◌ ● Ω ⇒ UNK.003 — Proof-near-claim and the trust surface AI engines need before they will cite you

## ⟡ Summary
The user named the destination ("convert attention into bookings," "rank," "get cited by ChatGPT/agents") but skipped the substrate all three gate on: verifiable proof co-located with the specific claim it backs. The non-obvious mechanic is that one structural move satisfies two different judges at once. A chunked retriever (AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity) lifts a claim and its adjacent evidence as a single passage, so a license number, named+dated review with verbatim detail, or response-time stat sitting INSIDE the service block travels with the claim and makes the source "safe to cite." The same adjacency de-risks a wary in-home buyer at the exact pixel where the decision happens. The corollary practitioners miss: this kills the conventional "Reviews" / "About" page pattern, where proof is one click away from every claim it should support — retriever and buyer both lose the link. Free-floating superlatives ("best," "trusted," "#1") are inert twice over: extractors can't ground them and skeptical local buyers discount them. Hard constraint specific to this domain: the proof atoms (actual license #, real insurance/bond, genuine reviews) must be supplied by the business and are subject to trade-specific jurisdictional display rules — they can never be invented, which makes the lint a two-stage check (presence of an evidence atom + audited authenticity of that atom), not a copywriting pass.

## ∴ Why it matters
AI engines route citations to sources they judge safe; buyers of in-home/in-person services convert on de-risking proof — both reward evidence co-located with the claim, not a separate 'reviews' page.

## ! Failure if missing
Pages read as generic marketing; AI engines cite a more evidenced competitor, and human visitors bounce because the de-risking proof (licensure, real reviews, guarantees) isn't where the decision is made.
