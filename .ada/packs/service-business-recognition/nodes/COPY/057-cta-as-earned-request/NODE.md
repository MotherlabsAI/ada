# ◇ ● ∴ κ ⇒ COPY.057 — CTA as Earned Request

- cluster: COPY · depth: L4 · truth: ∴ inference
- checkability: C3 (deterministic)
- compiles to: graph, wiki, c, claude, blueprint

**Summary.** A booking CTA borrows ALL its persuasive force from the proof and clarity above it — never from the button verb — so the page must have already earned the ask by the point of asking. Frontier nuances specific to local-service sites: (1) First-person, action-then-value phrasing wins — "Book My Free Estimate" beats "Submit" or "Contact Us" because "My" creates ownership and the verb names what the visitor RECEIVES, not the labor they perform. (2) One page asks for exactly ONE action, repeated verbatim at every commitment point (hero, post-value, post-proof, footer) plus a sticky mobile call-or-book bar; a menu of asks splits intent and flattens the funnel. (3) The CTA must pre-state what happens next ("we call back within 1 hour, no obligation") so the click is a known low-anxiety step, not a leap into a void where the visitor silently fears a salesperson will hound them. The load-bearing insight for local service: the click is almost never the conversion — the phone call or confirmed slot is — so the CTA is a promise about the next 60 seconds, and its real job is to remove last-inch hesitation, not to generate desire. Two enforceable consequences: the primary CTA verb is downstream of the business's true conversion channel (phone vs form vs calendar vs SMS), and single-action discipline is a lint rule (all primary CTA targets resolve to one action; banned-generic verbs rejected), not a copywriting suggestion.

**Why.** The CTA is where attention becomes a booking or evaporates. A page can rank and get cited and still convert nothing if the ask is generic ('Submit'), multiplied (five different asks), or anxiety-inducing (no statement of what follows). This node is the literal hinge of 'converts attention into bookings'.

**Failure if missing.** Visitors who are sold still don't act: 'Submit' frames effort not reward, competing CTAs scatter the decision, and an unexplained next-step ('then what — a salesperson hounds me?') manufactures last-second hesitation. Ranked, trusted traffic leaks out at the final inch.
