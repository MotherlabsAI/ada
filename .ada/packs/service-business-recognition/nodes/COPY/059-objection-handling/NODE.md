# ◇ ● ∴ ⇒ COPY.059 — Objection Handling

- cluster: COPY · depth: L4 · truth: ∴ inference
- checkability: C2 (rubric/LLM)
- compiles to: graph, wiki, c, claude, blueprint

**Summary.** Conversion copy for a local service business is the pre-emptive resolution of the 4-6 mundane unspoken objections that actually stall a booking: "Do you serve MY neighbourhood?", "What will this roughly cost?", "Are you licensed/insured?", "Can you come WHEN I need you?", "Will a stranger be respectful in my home?". Three non-obvious moves separate a page that converts from one that merely informs. First, positional resolution: each objection must be answered at the exact scroll-point where the doubt fires (price worry beside the pricing/CTA, trust worry beside the booking form, service-area note near the hero) — an answer placed three sections too late arrives after the abandon. Second, mined-not-invented: the objection set must come from real signals (sales-call transcripts, the questions people phone to ask, complaints in competitors' reviews), because a meeting-room guess optimizes the wrong doubts. Third, dual-reader payoff: the same self-contained Q/A that disarms a human is exactly the FAQPage/QAPage content ChatGPT and AI Overviews lift to answer "is X licensed / how much does X cost" — so objection handling in structured form serves the buyer and the agent reader from one artifact. The load-bearing risk: the highest-frequency objection (price) is also the one many trades refuse to answer publicly, so the page either publishes an honest range or must convert that refusal into a credible "here's why we quote on-site" answer rather than silence.

**Why.** Unhandled objections are the silent majority of lost bookings — the visitor never tells you why they left. Surfacing the real objection set and resolving each at its decision moment is the difference between a page that informs and a page that converts, and the same content feeds AEO/agent recognition.

**Failure if missing.** Visitors self-disqualify on questions the page could have answered in one sentence ('they probably don't cover my area', 'probably expensive', 'are they even licensed?'). The page looks complete but leaks every visitor whose specific doubt went unaddressed, and LLMs have no crisp Q/A to cite.
