# ◇ ● ∴ ⇒ COPY.060 — Proof Placement

## ⟡ Summary
Model proof as a layer threaded to objection peaks, not a "Testimonials" section. Two operating rules. (1) Objection-MATCHED placement: the single proof beside each primary CTA must rebut the objection firing at that CTA — price hesitation gets a value/cost testimonial; fear of a stranger in the home gets a courteous, on-time-technician one. A few decision-moment, objection-matched proofs beat a wall of generic praise stockpiled in a footer or logo row. (2) For a local business the proof that moves rank, humans, AND agents is the same canonical fact — Google rating/review count, years in business, license/insurance numbers, named local references — so author proof once as structured, reusable claims that simultaneously populate the trust strip near the booking form and feed Review/AggregateRating + LocalBusiness JSON-LD (the path to SERP star eligibility and agent-citable credibility). Decorative quotes do none of this; structured claims do all three. Caveat: self-serving AggregateRating markup is policy-restricted, so star eligibility depends on real, attributable review data existing.

## ∴ Why it matters
Proof placed at the decision moment is what converts a persuaded-but-cautious local buyer; proof modelled as structured claims is what earns review stars in the SERP and gives agents citable evidence. Misplaced proof (all at the bottom) is psychologically wasted and structurally invisible.

## ! Failure if missing
Testimonials sit on a separate page or footer where they reassure no one at the moment of doubt; reviews aren't structured so no star rating shows in search; agents find no citable evidence of credibility. The business looks unproven exactly where it needed to feel safe.
