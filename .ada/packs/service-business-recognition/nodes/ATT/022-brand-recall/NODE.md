# ◇ ● ∴ κ ⇒ ATT.022 — Brand Recall

- cluster: ATT · depth: L5 · truth: ∴ inference
- checkability: C4 (property-based)
- compiles to: graph, wiki, c, claude, blueprint

**Summary.** For a local-service business, recall is not logo-awareness but NAME-TO-NEED BINDING: when the pipe bursts at 11pm, does your name surface, and can it be re-found across the messy path local buyers actually take (saw the van -> Googled the name -> checked reviews -> asked ChatGPT 'is X plumber any good' -> booked)? Two non-obvious consequences. (1) Recall now depends on byte-identical NAP — exact business name, address, phone across homepage, Google Business Profile, LocalBusiness JSON-LD, and citations — because any drift FRAGMENTS THE ENTITY, breaking both human re-finding and the entity-resolution that lets Google and an LLM treat your scattered surfaces as one provider. A second location or a 'LLC' that appears in some places and not others quietly splits you into two weaker entities. (2) In the AEO era the recall HOLDER has moved: the buyer outsources memory to an agent, so being the name ChatGPT returns for '{service} in {city}' is the modern top-of-mind — and that slot is won by entity consistency, not ad spend. The homepage's job is to be the canonical, schema-marked source of the brand entity (sameAs links to GBP/socials, one constant NAP string, a distinctive verbal hook repeated in title tag/hero/footer) that both human memory and the model's entity graph can latch onto. The verifiable leverage point: the business name must be a single injected constant — never paraphrased — and lint-checked for byte-equality across H1, title, OG, schema, and footer. A pronounceable, locally-anchored name plus relentless consistency beats a clever logo, because consistency is the only part of recall the compiler can actually enforce.

**Why.** Recall is the bridge between the other clusters: attention won today only converts later if the name can be re-found at the moment of need. In 2026 the recall holder is often an LLM, so brand-entity consistency is simultaneously a memory, SEO, and AEO asset — fragmentation silently kills all three.

**Failure if missing.** Name/NAP drifts across homepage, GBP, and schema; the buyer who saw the van can't re-find you and ChatGPT can't resolve you as one entity, so a competitor with consistent citations gets recommended instead.
